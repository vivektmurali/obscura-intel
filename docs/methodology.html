The study

Do news-volume spikes predict abnormal returns, in the direction of tone?

The original framing behind this project — "every stock move has an event behind it" — is retrodictive and untestable: post-hoc narrative matching is free and worthless, and a large share of major price moves have no identifiable news driver at all. The question above is the only falsifiable, forward-running version, so it's the only one this study tests.

Design

  • Universe: 38 NSE mid/small-cap tickers, Nifty Midcap 100 + Smallcap 100, filtered for distinctive company names and price-history availability.
  • Events: a trailing-90-day vol_z z-score on GDELT article volume ≥ 3, with ≥ 5 articles that day; consecutive event days merged to the first. Direction is the sign of the equivalently-computed tone z-score.
  • Entry rule: close of the first NSE trading day strictly after the event's UTC date — never the event day itself.
  • Returns: market model (OLS vs ^NSEI) over a 120-trading-day window ending 21 days before entry; CAR at t+1, t+5, t+20.
  • Inference: one-sided permutation test (1,000 seeded draws) on the primary statistic, six secondary tests under Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction at q=0.10, one pre-registered robustness pass.

Full design lock: PREREGISTRATION.md, committed at 96b9c1e before any event data was joined to price data.

Result

Verdict: NULL. Primary test — mean(sign(tone_z) × CAR_t5) — came out -0.00151, one-sided permutation p = 0.763 (threshold p < 0.05). The point estimate has the wrong sign relative to the thesis. No secondary test survives BH-FDR (best: p = 0.238). The robustness pass doesn't change this.

Per the pre-registered kill criterion: "no detectable edge under this specification." No respecification is permitted now that results have been seen — this stands as the answer. Full numeric detail and figures in the v0.1 README.

Live pipeline

This site and its live feed run on a daily GitHub Actions cron: new GDELT volume/tone days are fetched, events recomputed over the full history, and each new event scored — intensity (news-volume percentile vs. the locked historical distribution) and novelty (days since that ticker's last event) only. Because the verdict is NULL, no forward-return number is ever shown or implied for a live event; that constraint is enforced in code, not just by convention.

Limitations

  • Survivorship bias: the universe is drawn from current index constituents, not constituents as of 2023.
  • Name-string entity matching is blunt: GDELT matching is exact-phrase string matching, not true entity resolution. A qualitative spot-check found 2-3 of the 20 largest historical spikes were contaminated by generic multi-company market-roundup articles.
  • Cross-sectional clustering: market-wide news days can inflate apparent significance. The pre-registered robustness pass found nothing to drop in this dataset, but the underlying risk remains disclosed.
  • Single market, single benchmark: NSE only, ^NSEI only.
  • GDELT coverage bias: crawl coverage favors certain outlets and languages.
  • Two tickers dropped (SAIL, BHEL) after persistent, unexplained GDELT fetch failures across three retry passes.
